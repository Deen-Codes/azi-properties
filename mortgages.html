Mortgage FAQs

Quick answers to the questions we hear most

For a fuller list covering deposits, fees, documents and timelines, see our FAQ page.

What mortgage is best for first-time buyers?

It depends on deposit size, income structure, credit profile and plans for the next few years. A first-time buyer with a 10% deposit on a £300,000 property often faces very different optimal lenders than someone with a 25% deposit. A short call narrows it quickly.

When should I start looking for a remortgage?

Six months before your current deal ends is the sweet spot. Most lenders let you lock in a new product that far ahead, so if rates fall you switch; if they rise, you're protected.

What is a buy-to-let mortgage?

A buy-to-let mortgage is for a property you rent out rather than live in. Affordability is assessed on the property's expected rental income against a stress-test interest rate, not your personal salary.

Can self-employed applicants get a mortgage?

Yes — on the same products as employed applicants, usually. Lenders typically want two or three years of accounts or SA302s, though some specialist lenders accept one year of accounts for the right case.
