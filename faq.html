First-Time Buyers

First-time buyer mortgage FAQs

How much deposit do I need for a mortgage?

The deposit needed depends on the property price, lender criteria and your circumstances. Many buyers start by understanding their savings, affordability and the type of mortgage they may qualify for.

Read our deposit guide

What is a mortgage in principle?

A mortgage in principle is an initial indication of what a lender may be willing to lend. It is not the same as a full mortgage offer, but it can help buyers understand their potential budget.

Read the guide

Can first-time buyers get mortgage advice before viewing properties?

Yes. Speaking to someone early can help you understand your budget, deposit position and what steps to take before making offers.

Affordability

Mortgage affordability FAQs

How much can I borrow for a mortgage?

How much you may be able to borrow depends on income, financial commitments, deposit size, credit history and lender criteria.

Read borrowing guide

How much do I need to earn for a mortgage?

There is no single salary requirement. Lenders usually assess income alongside spending, debts, deposit size and overall affordability.

Read income guide

What do lenders look at when I apply?

Lenders often review income, affordability, deposit, credit profile, employment structure and supporting documents.

Read lender criteria guide

Remortgages

Remortgage FAQs

When should I start looking for a remortgage?

Many homeowners review options before their current mortgage deal ends so they have time to compare routes and prepare documents.

Read remortgage timing guide

What happens when my fixed mortgage deal ends?

When a fixed mortgage deal ends, homeowners often move onto another rate or review remortgage options. Planning ahead can help avoid rushed decisions.

View remortgage support

Can I remortgage to release equity?

Some homeowners explore remortgaging to release equity, depending on property value, remaining mortgage balance and lender criteria.

Buy-to-Let

Buy-to-let mortgage FAQs

What is a buy-to-let mortgage?

A buy-to-let mortgage is designed for properties that are intended to be rented out rather than lived in by the borrower.

View buy-to-let mortgages

What do lenders look for in buy-to-let applications?

Lenders often consider rental income, deposit size, property type, investor profile and wider affordability.

Read buy-to-let lender guide

Can I remortgage a buy-to-let property?

Landlords may be able to remortgage a buy-to-let property depending on their circumstances, property value, rental income and lender criteria.

Explore buy-to-let support

Specialist Mortgages

Specialist mortgage FAQs

Can I get a mortgage if I am self-employed?

Self-employed applicants can get mortgages, but lenders may assess income differently and request documents such as accounts or tax records.

Read self-employed guide

What is a specialist mortgage?

A specialist mortgage may be relevant where circumstances are more complex, such as self-employment, unusual income, credit issues or non-standard property types.

View specialist mortgages

Do I need perfect credit to get a mortgage?

Not always. Credit profile matters, but lenders assess applications in different ways. The right route depends on the details of your situation.
