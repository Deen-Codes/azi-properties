Mortgage Basics

What do lenders look at when you apply for a mortgage?

Many buyers and homeowners want to know what lenders actually assess during a mortgage application. While each case is different, there are some common areas that often matter most.

Mortgage applications are not usually assessed on one factor alone. Lenders often build a wider picture of your financial position, borrowing needs and ability to manage repayments over time.

Understanding what lenders often look at can help you prepare more effectively and reduce uncertainty before starting the process.

Income and affordability

One of the main areas lenders often review is income. This helps them understand what level of borrowing may be realistic and how mortgage repayments might fit within your overall finances.

Affordability is usually broader than income alone. Regular spending, existing financial commitments and general budgeting can all play a role in how your application is assessed.

Credit profile

Lenders may also review your credit history as part of the application process. This can help them understand how you have managed borrowing and repayments in the past.

A credit profile is not always about perfection. In many cases, it is about understanding the wider picture and how your circumstances align with lender criteria.

Deposit size

Deposit size can influence how much you need to borrow and may affect the mortgage routes available to you. Buyers often explore deposit questions early because this can shape the overall application strategy.

The deposit is usually considered alongside affordability, property value and lender expectations rather than in isolation.

Employment and income structure

Lenders often look at how income is earned. For employed applicants, this may involve reviewing payslips or employment details. For self-employed applicants, the process may involve different documents and a broader look at income structure.

The aim is usually to understand how stable and sustainable the income appears in the context of a mortgage commitment.

Supporting documents

Mortgage applications often involve documents that help verify identity, income, spending and other key parts of your financial position. These can vary depending on the applicant and the type of mortgage being explored.

Preparing documents early can help make the process feel more organised and efficient.

Final thoughts

Lenders often look at a combination of income, affordability, credit history, deposit size and supporting documents when assessing a mortgage application.

If you understand these areas in advance, it becomes easier to prepare well and move into the mortgage process with more clarity and confidence.
