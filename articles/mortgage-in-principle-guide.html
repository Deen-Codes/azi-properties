Mortgage Basics

What is a mortgage in principle?

A mortgage in principle can help buyers understand what they may be able to borrow before making an offer on a property.

A mortgage in principle, sometimes called an agreement in principle or decision in principle, is an initial indication from a lender of how much they may be willing to lend based on the information provided.

It is not usually a guaranteed mortgage offer, but it can be a useful step when preparing to buy a property.

Why buyers use a mortgage in principle

Many buyers use a mortgage in principle to get a clearer idea of their potential borrowing before viewing properties or making an offer.

It can also help show estate agents or sellers that you have started the mortgage process and are taking the purchase seriously.

Is a mortgage in principle the same as a mortgage offer?

No. A mortgage in principle is not the same as a full mortgage offer. It is usually based on an initial assessment and does not always include the full checks required for final approval.

A full mortgage offer normally comes later, after a more detailed application, documentation review and property assessment.

What information might be needed?

To obtain a mortgage in principle, you may need to provide basic details about your income, deposit, employment, spending and financial commitments.

The exact information required can vary depending on the lender and your circumstances.

Can a mortgage in principle affect your credit score?

Some lenders may use a soft credit search, while others may use a hard credit search. The type of check can depend on the lender and the application route.

This is one reason why it can be useful to understand the process before applying.

How long does a mortgage in principle last?

A mortgage in principle is usually valid for a limited period. The exact length can depend on the lender.

If it expires before you find a property, you may need to review or refresh the information.

Should first-time buyers get one?

Many first-time buyers find a mortgage in principle useful because it gives them a clearer idea of budget and borrowing before making serious property decisions.

It can also help buyers feel more prepared when speaking to estate agents or beginning the search process.

Final thoughts

A mortgage in principle can be a helpful early step in the home-buying journey. It gives an indication of potential borrowing, but it should not be treated as a guaranteed mortgage offer.

If you are preparing to buy, understanding the role of a mortgage in principle can help you approach the process with more clarity and confidence.
