Remortgages

What happens when your fixed mortgage deal ends?

When your fixed-rate mortgage ends, your deal doesn’t disappear — but what happens next can impact your monthly payments significantly.

When your fixed mortgage deal ends, you don’t lose your mortgage — but your rate will usually change. This is often the point where homeowners start reviewing their options.

You move onto the lender’s standard variable rate (SVR)

Most lenders will automatically move you onto their standard variable rate (SVR) once your fixed deal expires.

The SVR is typically higher than fixed deals and can change at any time, meaning your monthly payments may increase.

Your monthly payments may rise

If your new rate is higher than your previous fixed rate, your monthly repayments can increase. This is why many homeowners look to secure a new deal before their current one ends.

You can remortgage to a new deal

One option is to remortgage, which means switching to a new mortgage deal — either with your current lender or a different one.

This can allow you to:

  • Secure a new fixed rate
  • Potentially reduce monthly payments
  • Adjust your mortgage term
  • Release equity if needed

When should you start looking?

Many homeowners begin reviewing their options several months before their fixed deal ends. This gives time to explore rates and secure a new deal ready for when the current one finishes.

Starting early can help avoid moving onto a higher SVR unnecessarily.

Can you stay with your current lender?

Yes — this is often called a product transfer. You switch to a new deal with your existing lender without fully remortgaging.

This can sometimes be quicker and involve less paperwork, though it may not always offer the most competitive rates.

Things to consider before choosing your next deal

When reviewing your options, it’s worth thinking about:

  • Your current financial situation
  • Interest rate trends
  • How long you plan to stay in the property
  • Flexibility vs fixed certainty

Final thoughts

The end of a fixed mortgage deal is not a problem — it’s an opportunity to review your options.

Taking action early can help you stay in control of your payments and find a deal that better suits your situation.

Frequently asked questions

What happens if I do nothing when my fixed rate ends?

You will usually move onto your lender’s standard variable rate, which is often higher.

How early can I remortgage?

Many lenders allow you to secure a new deal several months before your current one ends.

Is remortgaging always the best option?

Not always. It depends on your situation — sometimes staying with your lender may be suitable.

Will my credit score affect remortgaging?

It can influence your options and rates, so it’s something lenders may consider.

Quick summary

  • Fixed deal ends → move to SVR
  • Payments may increase
  • Remortgage for better deal
  • Start reviewing early
