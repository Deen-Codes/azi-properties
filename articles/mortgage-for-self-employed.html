Mortgage Guide

Can you get a mortgage if you are self-employed?

Self-employed applicants can still access mortgages, but the process may involve different checks compared to employed applicants.

Being self-employed does not mean you cannot get a mortgage. However, lenders often assess income differently compared to applicants in full-time employment.

The key difference is usually how income is verified and how consistent it appears over time.

How lenders assess self-employed income

Instead of relying on payslips, lenders often look at financial records such as accounts, tax calculations or other documentation to understand income.

This helps them assess how stable and sustainable earnings may be over time.

Why consistency matters

Consistency of income can be an important factor in mortgage applications. Lenders may look at how income has performed over recent years to build a clearer picture.

Fluctuating income does not automatically prevent a mortgage, but it may be assessed more carefully.

What documents might be required?

Self-employed applicants are often asked for documents that help demonstrate income. These may include tax returns, accounts or other financial records.

The exact documents required can vary depending on the lender and your circumstances.

Deposit and affordability

As with other mortgage types, deposit size and affordability are still important. Lenders usually consider the full financial picture, including commitments and outgoings.

A larger deposit may provide access to different options, but it does not replace the need for affordability checks.

Credit history still plays a role

Credit history can also form part of the assessment process. Lenders may review your financial track record alongside your income.

This is usually considered as part of the wider application rather than as a standalone factor.

Preparation can make a difference

Preparing early can help make the process feel smoother. This may include organising financial documents, reviewing income records and understanding your current financial position.

The more clearly your income can be presented, the easier it may be for lenders to assess your application.

Final thoughts

Self-employed applicants can still access mortgages, but the process often involves a more detailed review of income and financial records.

Understanding what lenders may look for can help you approach the process with more clarity and confidence.
