Mortgage Rates Guide

What are the best mortgage rates in the UK?

Mortgage rates change frequently, and the “best” rate often depends on your deposit, income, and overall financial profile.

When people search for the best mortgage rates in the UK, they are often looking for the lowest available interest rate. While this is understandable, the reality is that the best rate is not always the same for everyone.

Mortgage rates vary based on your circumstances, the type of mortgage, and wider market conditions.

What are current mortgage rates in the UK?

Mortgage rates move regularly depending on economic conditions. As of 2026, average fixed mortgage rates are typically around 5–6%, although lower rates may be available depending on deposit size and borrower profile. Some of the lowest deals on the market can fall below this range, but these are often available to borrowers with larger deposits and stronger financial profiles. Why mortgage rates change

Mortgage rates are influenced by several factors, including the Bank of England base rate, inflation and wider economic conditions. [oai_citation:2‡Money.co.uk](https://www.money.co.uk/mortgages/bank-of-england-base-rate?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Even when the base rate stays the same, mortgage rates can still rise or fall based on market expectations and lender decisions.

What affects the rate you are offered?

The rate you are offered is usually based on your individual circumstances rather than just market averages.

Key factors often include:

  • Deposit size
  • Income and affordability
  • Credit history
  • Property type
  • Loan-to-value (LTV)

Fixed vs variable rates

Fixed-rate mortgages keep your interest rate the same for a set period, while variable or tracker mortgages can change over time.

Choosing between them depends on whether you prefer stability or flexibility.

Why the lowest rate is not always the best

A low interest rate may look attractive, but it is important to consider the full mortgage deal. Fees, flexibility and long-term suitability can all matter just as much as the rate itself.

The best mortgage is usually one that fits your situation, not just the lowest number on comparison tables.

How to find the best mortgage rate

Many buyers and homeowners compare rates across lenders or speak to a mortgage adviser to understand what is available to them.

Brokers often review a wide range of lenders and deals, helping you find options that match your circumstances rather than relying on general comparisons. [oai_citation:3‡MoneySavingExpert.com](https://www.moneysavingexpert.com/mortgages/best-mortgages-cashback/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Final thoughts

The best mortgage rate in the UK depends on your personal situation, the type of mortgage and current market conditions.

Understanding how rates work can help you make more informed decisions and approach the process with greater confidence.
