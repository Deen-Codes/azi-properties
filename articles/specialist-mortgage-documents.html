Specialist Mortgage Guide

What documents might you need for a specialist mortgage?

Specialist cases are won on paperwork. The earlier the right documents are gathered, the faster an application moves — and the more lenders will consider it.

Specialist mortgages cover a wide range of situations: self-employed income, contractor day-rates, complex earnings, adverse credit history, non-standard construction, HMOs, expats, foreign nationals and high-net-worth lending. Every lender has their own expectations, but the documents below are the ones most commonly requested across specialist desks.

Personal identification and address

  • Valid passport or UK driving licence
  • Two to three months of recent utility bills, council tax statement or bank statements as proof of address
  • For non-UK nationals: proof of right to reside (visa, BRP or settled status documentation)

Self-employed sole traders and partnerships

  • Two to three years of SA302s from HMRC
  • Matching tax year overviews for the same period
  • Three to six months of business and personal bank statements
  • An accountant's reference or finalised accounts, where available

Some specialist lenders will consider one year of accounts, or a projection signed off by a qualified accountant, for cases where the business is newer but clearly established.

Limited company directors

  • Two to three years of finalised company accounts
  • Personal SA302s and tax year overviews
  • A breakdown of salary and dividends drawn
  • Recent business bank statements

A handful of specialist lenders assess limited company directors on their share of retained profit rather than just salary plus dividends — which often increases borrowing meaningfully. Knowing which lenders do this is usually half the value of broker advice on these cases.

Contractors and day-rate workers

  • Current signed contract with daily/weekly rate
  • Evidence of a track record — usually six to twelve months of contracting history
  • Recent bank statements showing income receipts
  • CV if requested by the underwriter

Many lenders now assess contractors on a "day-rate × 5 × 46 weeks" basis rather than self-employed accounts, which is usually a more generous calculation.

CIS (Construction Industry Scheme) workers

  • Six to twelve months of CIS payslips or remittance advices
  • Matching bank statements
  • Self-assessment returns if available

Adverse credit cases

If there's been a default, missed payment, CCJ, IVA, debt management plan or bankruptcy on your record, specialist lenders will typically want:

  • A full, recent credit file (Experian, Equifax or TransUnion)
  • A written explanation of each adverse event and how it was resolved
  • Evidence of settled accounts where relevant
  • Proof that the underlying cause is no longer present

A clear, honest credit narrative — ideally prepared with a broker before submission — usually moves an adverse case through underwriting faster than the documents alone.

Non-standard property

If the property itself is the unusual element — timber frame, concrete construction, above commercial premises, thatched, listed, high-rise or multi-unit — expect additional evidence:

  • Full structural or homebuyer's survey
  • Building insurance confirmation
  • EPC certificate
  • For flats: lease documentation, service charge and ground rent evidence
  • For HMOs: licence, floorplans and tenancy schedule

Expats and foreign nationals

  • Passport and visa / right-to-reside documentation
  • International bank statements covering the income source
  • Employer reference translated to English if required
  • Evidence of the deposit's origin, often with translated documentation
  • Currency conversion records where income is paid in a non-GBP currency

Buy-to-let portfolio landlords

  • Portfolio schedule listing every property, outstanding balance, monthly rent and product end date
  • Business plan or cash flow forecast if asked
  • Background affordability evidence across the portfolio
  • For SPVs: company accounts, SIC codes matching buy-to-let activity and director information

Deposit evidence

Any specialist case benefits from a clear paper trail for the deposit:

  • Bank statements showing accumulation from salary / business income
  • Sale completion statements if from a property sale
  • Signed gifted deposit letter if from family
  • Loan documentation if any portion is borrowed

Final thoughts

Specialist mortgage applications run on evidence. Having the right documents gathered, organised and reviewed before submission is usually the single biggest determinant of how smoothly the case moves. A short pre-application conversation is almost always worth the time — it narrows the lender list, sets the exact document requirements, and avoids a late request that delays exchange.
