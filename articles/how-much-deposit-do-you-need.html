First-Time Buyer Guide

How much deposit do you need for a house in the UK?

One of the most common questions first-time buyers ask is how much deposit they need before applying for a mortgage. The answer can depend on the lender, the property and your overall situation.

A deposit is the amount of money you contribute upfront towards the purchase of a property. The mortgage then covers the remaining amount, subject to lender approval and affordability checks.

In simple terms, the bigger your deposit, the smaller the percentage you may need to borrow. This can sometimes improve the range of mortgage options available to you, although it is not the only factor lenders look at.

Why deposit size matters

Deposit size can affect the loan-to-value ratio, often shortened to LTV. This is the percentage of the property value that you are borrowing compared with the amount you are putting down yourself.

For example, a buyer contributing a larger deposit may be borrowing a lower percentage of the property price. In some cases, that can lead to broader lender choice or more competitive mortgage products.

Do all buyers need the same deposit?

No. The amount needed can vary depending on your lender, the property type, your credit profile, your income and the mortgage product being considered.

Some buyers focus on reaching a minimum deposit level just to begin exploring mortgage options, while others may prefer to save more in order to strengthen their position before applying.

What should first-time buyers consider besides the deposit?

A deposit is important, but it is not the only cost involved in buying a property. Buyers should also think about legal fees, surveys, moving costs and any other expenses that may come with the purchase.

It is also worth understanding how monthly mortgage repayments could fit into your wider budget. Lenders will usually look at affordability, not just whether you have saved a deposit.

How can buyers prepare more effectively?

A useful starting point is to understand your current savings, income, spending and financial commitments. This can help you build a clearer picture of what mortgage route might realistically suit your situation.

Buyers often benefit from understanding deposit expectations alongside affordability, documentation and the overall mortgage process, rather than focusing on just one number in isolation.

Final thoughts

There is no one-size-fits-all answer to how much deposit you need for a house in the UK. The right amount depends on your circumstances and the type of mortgage you are exploring.

If you are unsure where to start, clearer mortgage guidance can help you understand what level of deposit may fit your plans and what the next step should look like.
