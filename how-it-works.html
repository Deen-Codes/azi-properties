How It Works

A clearer, calmer route to the right mortgage

The mortgage process shouldn't feel rushed or transactional. From your first no-obligation conversation through to offer and completion, AZI Mortgages guides you through each stage with structure, transparency and a focus on the detail that matters.

What to expect from start to finish

Most clients come to us with a clear sense of their goal, but less clarity on the steps between here and there. Our role is to close that gap — translating lender criteria, product choice and process into something you can see clearly and act on with confidence.

Every journey is slightly different, but the structure below explains how a typical AZI Mortgages mortgage conversation unfolds.

The initial consultation is free of charge and with no obligation. If you choose to proceed, the research, recommendation and application work is a paid service — and any fee is agreed in writing before you commit.

At a glance

  • Free, no-obligation first conversation
  • Whole-of-market research across UK lenders
  • A recommendation tailored to your circumstances
  • Support through application and offer
  • Fees agreed in writing before any work begins

The AZI Mortgages process

Six structured stages, designed to keep you informed and in control at every step.

Step 1

Initial conversation

We start with a relaxed, no-obligation call to understand your goals, timing and any constraints. You leave with a clear view of what happens next — and whether we're the right fit.

Step 2

Fact-find & document review

We take a closer look at income, deposit, credit position, existing commitments and the property you have in mind. This is where the detail starts to shape the advice.

Step 3

Whole-of-market research

We review products across a broad panel of UK lenders — including high street names and specialist providers — and filter those that genuinely fit your position.

Step 4

Recommendation & decision in principle

You receive a clear, written recommendation explaining the chosen product, rate, fees and trade-offs. Where helpful, we secure a decision in principle so you can move on a property with confidence.

Step 5

Application & offer

We prepare and submit the full application, liaise with the lender, and keep you updated through underwriting, valuation and formal mortgage offer.

Step 6

Completion & ongoing review

Once you've completed, we stay in touch. Before your product ends — or your circumstances change — we'll reach out so your next decision is planned, not rushed.

Why this process works

A structured process isn't just about neatness. It's what makes the difference between a good outcome and a rushed one.

No pressure, no surprises

We explain every fee, every trade-off and every next step in writing. If something isn't right, we'll say so early.

Whole-of-market access

We're not tied to a handful of lenders. That breadth often matters most for complex income, higher LTVs or specialist cases.

One point of contact

You deal with a single adviser from enquiry through to completion — not a rotating call centre.

Ongoing relationship

A mortgage is a long-term commitment. We treat the advice that way, reviewing your position well before the next decision point.

How It Works FAQs

Common questions about the process

Is the first conversation really free?

Yes. The initial consultation is free of charge and with no obligation. Beyond the initial call, full research, recommendation and application work is a paid service — any fee is agreed in writing before you commit, so there are no surprises.

How long does the process take?

From first call to mortgage offer, a straightforward case often completes in four to six weeks, subject to the lender, valuation and your own timing. More complex cases can take longer — we'll give you a realistic timeline upfront.

Which lenders do you work with?

We research across a broad panel of UK residential, buy-to-let and specialist lenders. That includes major high street names alongside providers who focus on self-employed, complex income or adverse credit cases.

What do you need from me to get started?

Very little at first — just a clear picture of your goals and timing. Documents such as payslips, bank statements, identification and credit information come into play once we're ready to shape a firm recommendation.

What happens after completion?

We stay in touch. Well before your fixed period ends, we'll reach out with a remortgage review so you're never rolled onto a lender's standard variable rate by default.
