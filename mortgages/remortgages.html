Whole-of-market remortgage advice. We compare your existing lender's retention offer against the full UK panel on total cost, not just headline rate, and lock in your new product up to six months before your current deal ends.

The cost of a badly timed remortgage is real

When a fixed rate ends, most lenders move the loan to their Standard Variable Rate (SVR) — often 3–4 percentage points higher than the fixed product. On a £200,000 balance, that can mean an overnight rise of £300–£500 a month. Staying on SVR for even two or three months can cost more than a full year's advice fee.

We work to a six-month runway. Most high-street lenders let you reserve a new product that far ahead — so if rates fall, we switch; if they rise, you're already protected. Your existing lender's retention offer is compared against the whole market on total cost over the fix period (rate + product fee + valuation), not headline rate alone.

For equity release, debt consolidation or capital raising on the property, the remortgage conversation widens — we'll show the affordability impact clearly before anything is submitted.

Four reasons homeowners come to us for a remortgage

Each changes which lender is right for you — and each can be handled in a single structured review.

Fixed Rate Ending Soon

Your deal expires in the next six months. We benchmark your lender's retention offer against the wider market and reserve a product now so SVR never kicks in.

Capital Raising

Extension, kitchen, deposit for a buy-to-let, consolidating expensive unsecured debt. We model the monthly impact before submission and confirm which lenders are comfortable with the purpose.

Changing LTV Band

Your property has likely risen in value since your last deal. Moving from 85% LTV to 75% LTV can unlock materially better rates — we recalculate the current loan-to-value properly.

Restructuring the Loan

Splitting into part repayment / part interest-only, adjusting the term, or moving a buy-to-let into a limited company structure — all handled in the same conversation.

The remortgage process

While every case is different, many remortgage journeys follow a similar structure.

Step 1

Benchmark the retention offer

Your existing lender's best renewal product, set against the five or six most competitive whole-of-market alternatives at your LTV and term.

Step 2

Reserve the new product

Most lenders let us lock in the new deal up to six months ahead. If rates drop before your switch date, we re-rate; if they rise, you're already priced in.

Step 3

Submit & manage the case

We handle the full application, lender queries and (for external switches) solicitor instruction on the legal transfer.

Step 4

Switch on the right day

The new product starts the day after your current deal ends — no SVR exposure, no gap.

Remortgage FAQs

Questions homeowners often ask

When exactly should I start looking?

Six months before your current deal ends is the sweet spot. Most lenders let you reserve a new product that far ahead, and many allow a free re-rate if market rates drop between reservation and switch date.

Product transfer or full remortgage — what's the difference?

A product transfer stays with your existing lender, needs minimal paperwork and is usually quickest. A remortgage moves to a new lender, opens the whole market and can save significantly — but requires a fresh affordability assessment and solicitor involvement.

Can I remortgage to release equity?

Yes — if your property has risen in value and your affordability supports a larger loan. Common uses: home improvements, deposit for a second property, or consolidating higher-cost unsecured debt. We'll model the revised monthly cost against the total interest over the term before recommending.

Will affordability be checked again?

For a product transfer with your existing lender, usually no additional affordability is needed. For an external remortgage — or any capital raise — a full affordability assessment applies, including the stress-test rate.

Will I have to pay an Early Repayment Charge?

If you exit a fixed deal before its end date, yes — typically 1–5% of the balance, stepping down each year. We time external remortgages to the day after the ERC period ends, so there's never a charge.
