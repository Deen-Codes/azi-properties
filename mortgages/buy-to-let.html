Buy-to-let lending has changed — advice needs to as well

Since Section 24 phased out mortgage interest relief on personally-held BTLs, more investors have moved new purchases into a limited-company SPV structure. It isn't the right answer for everyone — personal tax band, holding timeline, existing portfolio and exit strategy all feed into the decision — but it's now a conversation every serious landlord needs to have.

Affordability is assessed on the Interest Cover Ratio (ICR), not your salary. Lenders typically stress-test rental income at 125–145% of the mortgage payment at a notional rate (often 5.5–8%). A property producing £1,200 a month in rent may support more or less borrowing depending on which lender runs which stress test.

Whether this is your first purchase, a fourth property taking you into "portfolio landlord" territory (four or more mortgaged BTLs), or a restructure of an existing portfolio, we run the case against the lenders that actually match your profile.

The buy-to-let mortgage process

While every investor journey is different, the process often follows a few key stages.

Step 1

Structure & tax discussion

Personal name vs SPV, your tax band, exit strategy and how the new property sits in the wider portfolio. Where needed, we coordinate with your accountant.

Step 2

ICR-based borrowing analysis

We model expected rent against multiple lender stress tests to find the maximum useful borrowing — not just the maximum possible.

Step 3

Lender selection & submission

The shortlist is filtered by fee structure, valuation treatment of the property type, SPV appetite and speed of underwriting — then we submit.

Step 4

Completion & portfolio review

Through valuation, offer and completion — plus an annual portfolio review to flag remortgage windows and restructuring opportunities.

Buy-to-Let FAQs

Questions landlords and investors often ask

What deposit do I need for a buy-to-let mortgage?

Most BTL products start at 75% LTV, meaning a 25% deposit. A smaller pool of lenders will go to 80%, and a further few to 85%, but pricing tightens significantly above 75% LTV. On a £200,000 property, expect a £50,000 deposit as the working number.

Should I buy in a limited company (SPV) or in my own name?

It depends on your tax band, intention to hold long-term, and whether rental income pushes you into a higher bracket. Broadly: higher-rate taxpayers holding long-term often benefit from SPV; basic-rate or short-hold investors often don't. This is a tax question as much as a mortgage one — we'll map it out properly before you commit.

How does the ICR stress test actually work?

Lenders divide expected monthly rent by the mortgage payment at a notional stress rate (often 5.5–8%). The resulting ratio needs to clear 125% for basic-rate BTLs and 145% for higher-rate or company structures. If the rent doesn't stress, a larger deposit, a different lender, or a longer initial fix can all bring the case back in range.

What's the Stamp Duty surcharge on a second property?

In England, buy-to-let and second-home purchases carry a 3% surcharge on top of standard SDLT rates — usually 5% higher than the first-time-buyer rate at every band. A £200,000 BTL therefore incurs around £7,500 in SDLT. We confirm the exact figure early so deposit planning is accurate.

I have four or more BTL mortgages — does that change things?

Yes. You're classified a "portfolio landlord" under PRA rules, which means any new application is stress-tested against your whole portfolio, not just the new property. Some lenders exit this space entirely; others specialise in it. We select accordingly.
