Moving Home Mortgages

Keep the chain moving — and the finance confirmed early

Whether you're porting your existing deal, switching to a better product, or restructuring the borrowing around the next property, a named adviser manages the case end-to-end so the mortgage is never the reason a sale falls through.

Three routes — one decision that sets your monthly payment for years

A second-mover has three real options. Port your current mortgage deal to the new property (keeping the rate, avoiding ERCs), port and top up with additional borrowing at the lender's current rates on the new money, or redeem and remortgage to a whole-of-market alternative if the exit penalty is worth it. We'll model all three on total cost.

Timing matters. Most lenders require the port to complete within 90–180 days of the sale. Your income, credit and property will be reassessed against current affordability rules — even if your existing loan passed a stricter test back in 2019, it won't automatically pass today's.

On top of the mortgage, you're budgeting for Stamp Duty (with a higher rate if you own another property at completion, even briefly), solicitor fees, survey, valuation and the estate agent on your sale. We'll run through the full picture so the numbers aren't a late surprise.

The four moving-home questions that drive the biggest savings

Each one can shift the total cost of the move by thousands. We run all four before recommending a route.

Port, Top Up, or Remortgage?

A port keeps your fixed rate but ties you to your current lender. Redeeming and remortgaging opens the whole market — but may trigger an ERC. We compare total cost of both over the fix period.

New Borrowing Level

Upsizing means new affordability, new deposit from equity released on the sale, and new LTV band. We calculate the real borrowing figure today's lenders will support — not the number from five years ago.

Stamp Duty Impact

The SDLT bill on your next purchase is often the second-largest cost after the deposit. We confirm the exact figure — including surcharges if you'll briefly own two properties — before you commit.

Chain Protection

Mortgage offers typically last three to six months. If your chain drags, we manage extensions with the lender or re-apply before the offer lapses — keeping the move alive.

The moving home mortgage process

While every situation is different, many home movers benefit from a similar step-by-step approach.

Step 1

Work out the real budget

Equity released from the sale, new borrowing, SDLT, fees and buffer. One clear affordability number before you start viewings.

Step 2

Port-or-switch decision

We compare the cost of porting (keeping your current rate, topping up at current rates on new borrowing) against a clean remortgage, including any ERC.

Step 3

DIP & offer submission

A full Decision in Principle with the right lender, followed by full application and underwriting once your offer on the new property is accepted.

Step 4

Exchange & completion

We manage lender, solicitor and sale-side timings in parallel so the mortgage offer, exchange and keys line up cleanly.

Moving Home FAQs

Questions home movers often ask

Can I take my current mortgage to the new property?

Most residential mortgages are portable in principle, but porting isn't automatic — you still need to pass current affordability, and the property has to meet the lender's criteria. Porting makes most sense when you're mid-way through a fixed rate that's materially better than today's market pricing.

Will I have to pay an Early Repayment Charge?

Only if you redeem outside your current deal without porting — ERCs typically run 1–5% of the outstanding balance. On a £250,000 loan in a 3% ERC period, that's £7,500. We calculate whether the better market rate justifies paying it, or whether porting is cheaper overall.

How does Stamp Duty work on my next purchase?

Standard SDLT rates apply: nil to £250,000, 5% to £925,000, 10% to £1.5m, 12% above. If you still own your current home at completion, the 3% surcharge applies to the whole purchase — though it's refundable if you sell the first home within 36 months.

My mortgage offer expires — what happens if the chain drags?

Most offers are valid for three to six months. If the chain slips, we apply for an extension with the lender or, if the offer has lapsed, we re-run the case quickly with minimal disruption. Worst case, we switch lenders — we don't lose the move.

What if I want to complete on my new home before the sale of my current one?

This is where bridging finance or a let-to-buy remortgage may come in. Both are specialist areas with short terms and higher rates, but for the right case they can protect the move. We'll show costs against waiting for the sale to complete first.
