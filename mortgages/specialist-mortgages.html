Specialist Mortgages

Complex cases placed with the lenders that actually do them

Self-employed, contractor, complex-income, adverse credit, non-standard construction, expats and high-net-worth — all cases where the high street says no and a specialist lender routinely says yes. We know the difference.

High-street "no" is rarely the final answer

Most high-street lenders work to narrow, automated criteria. If your income is bonus-heavy, paid in a foreign currency, just under a year old as a sole trader, or your credit file has a historic default, an online decision will often come back as a no — even when the underlying case is strong.

Specialist lenders exist for exactly these cases. They underwrite manually, price for the risk rather than reject outright, and accept the kind of evidence a high-street lender won't look at. The difference between a decline and a mortgage offer is usually which lender the case was submitted to — not whether the case itself is viable.

We've placed cases for contractors paid in USD, LTD directors drawing on retained profit, applicants with a recent CCJ now satisfied, and buyers of timber-frame or steel-frame non-standard construction. The question is always: which lender on the panel is actually comfortable with this profile today?

The four specialist profiles where adviser choice matters most

Each has its own lender panel, its own preferred documents, and its own common rejection reasons.

Self-Employed & LTD Directors

Lenders split sharply on whether to use net profit, salary + dividend, or retained profit. A £60k gross figure can produce very different affordability from one lender to the next.

Contractors & Variable Income

Day-rate contract assessment (rate × 5 × 46), zero-hours averages, bonus/commission weighting, foreign-currency haircuts — we match the right lender to how your income actually works.

Credit Rebuild

Defaults, CCJs, DMPs and historic IVAs are workable with the right lender. Timing since the event, whether it's satisfied, and the reason behind it all feed into the decision.

Non-Standard Property

Timber frame, steel frame, concrete (Wimpey No-Fines, Cornish Unit), above commercial, studio flats, high-rise, short-lease — each needs a lender that accepts the construction type and valuation basis.

The specialist mortgage process

A more complex case often benefits from a clear and structured process.

Step 1

Full case assessment

We look at your full position — income structure, tax evidence, credit file, deposit source and property — before any lender is approached. No wasted soft-searches.

Step 2

Lender shortlist & soft decision

We agree a lender directly with the broker desk — often through a "pre-DIP" confirmation — before a credit footprint is placed. This is the key step on specialist cases.

Step 3

Packaged submission

Specialist underwriters want the full story on paper: accountant's reference, contract copies, credit event explanations, construction reports. We package the case so it lands ready-to-approve.

Step 4

Offer & completion

We manage the case through valuation, underwriting queries and formal offer — and stay the point of contact through to completion.

Specialist Mortgage FAQs

Questions clients often ask

Can I get a mortgage with only one year of self-employed accounts?

Yes, with the right lender. Most high-street lenders want two or three years, but a handful of specialist lenders accept one year of SA302s and tax year overviews — particularly if the trading history is backed by a strong accountant's reference and the figures are stable.

I'm a day-rate contractor — how is my income assessed?

Contractor-friendly lenders assess income as day rate × 5 days × 46–48 weeks, treating that as annualised gross income. A £500-a-day contractor is typically viewed as earning £115,000 a year, regardless of company structure — which can support far more borrowing than drawing salary + dividend would suggest.

Can I get a mortgage with a default or CCJ on my credit file?

Often yes, depending on the amount, when it was registered, and whether it's now satisfied. A single £500 default from four years ago on a satisfied credit file will be workable with a near-prime lender at a small rate premium. Multiple recent events narrow the panel but don't always rule lending out.

Does my LTD company's retained profit count towards affordability?

With a subset of lenders, yes. Instead of assessing only salary + dividend taken, these lenders use salary + net profit retained in the company — which can double or triple the apparent income. Critical for directors who deliberately leave profit in the company for tax efficiency.

Will a non-standard construction property limit my options?

It narrows the panel rather than closing it. Timber frame, steel frame and specific concrete builds each have lender lists that accept them. A Level 3 RICS survey or EWS1 (for flats) often unlocks further options. We'll confirm which lenders are realistic before submission.
